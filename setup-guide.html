Your New Blog:
3 Small Jobs to Finish

About 15 minutes in total

Hi Graeme,

Good news. Your blog is working. It writes articles about boilers and posts them by itself.

There are 3 small jobs left. Only you can do them, because they need your logins.

Before you start, have these ready:

  • Your bank card
  • Your Webflow login
  • Your Names.co.uk login (where your web address is kept)
  • Your Google login
JOB 1 of 3

Set up automatic posting

⏱ Takes about 5 minutes

Why: the "Pro" plan lets the blog run fully on its own. It writes and posts new articles, with no one pressing any buttons.

These are the steps:

  1. Go to app.trysight.ai and log in.
  2. Find the Billing page. It is in the settings menu.
  3. Add your bank card.
  4. Pick the plan called Pro.
Good to know: Pro costs $129 US a month. That is about £100.

That's it. Andrew will turn on the autopilot after this is done.

This one is your choice: the Pro plan is only needed for automatic posting. If you would rather press the post button yourself for each article, you can skip this job and stay on the cheaper plan.
JOB 2 of 3

Put the blog on your web address

⏱ Takes about 5 minutes, plus a short wait

Why: right now the blog lives on a free web address. Moving it to blog.commercialboilersolutions.co.uk helps you show up on Google.

Don't worry: this will NOT touch your main website. We are only adding one new thing. Nothing gets changed or removed.

First, in Webflow:

  1. Go to webflow.com and log in.
  2. Open your site, Commercial Boiler Solutions.
  3. Click the gear wheel ⚙️ to open Site settings.
  4. Click the tab called Publishing.
  5. Click Add custom domain.
  6. Type this in, exactly as written: blog.commercialboilersolutions.co.uk
  7. Keep this page open. You will come back to it.

Next, in Names.co.uk:

  1. Open a new tab. Go to names.co.uk and log in.
  2. Find your domain: commercialboilersolutions.co.uk.
  3. Click DNS settings. It may be called Advanced DNS.
  4. Click Add record (or "Add new record").
  5. Fill in the 3 boxes like this: Type: CNAME Name: blog Value: proxy-ssl.webflow.com
  6. Click Save.
Important: only ADD this one new record. Do not change or delete anything else on that page. The other records run your website and email.

Last, back in Webflow:

  1. Go and have a cup of tea. The internet needs about 30 minutes to catch up.
  2. Go back to the Webflow page from before.
  3. Click Check status next to your new web address.
You will see: a green tick or the word "Connected". If it's not green yet, wait a bit longer and try again.
  1. Click the big Publish button.

Done! Your blog now lives on your own web address.

JOB 3 of 3

Let Google find your blog

⏱ Takes about 3 minutes — do this AFTER Job 2

Why: this tells Google your blog exists. Without it, the articles sit there and Google never looks at them.

  1. Go back to app.trysight.ai.
  2. Find the button that says Connect Google Search Console. It is in the settings area.
  3. Click it. A Google sign-in window will open.
  4. Sign in with your Google account.
  5. Click Allow when Google asks.
You will see: the page says "Connected". That means Google and your blog are now talking to each other.

That's everything! 🎉

Your blog now writes itself, posts itself, and tells Google about it — all on its own.
